# GAAP Hierarchy and the ASC Codification Structure

## Core Idea

The **FASB Accounting Standards Codification (ASC)** is the single authoritative source of U.S. Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) for nongovernmental entities. Before the Codification existed, practitioners had to search across dozens of separate pronouncements. The ASC collapsed all of that into one numbered framework, making the hierarchy of authority unambiguous: **if it is in the Codification, it is GAAP; if it is not, it is not GAAP**.

The GAAP hierarchy under ASC 105 recognizes two tiers:

| Tier | Source |
|------|--------|
| **Authoritative** | All content within the ASC itself |
| **Non-authoritative** | FASB Staff Q&As, industry practice, accounting textbooks, etc. |

When no authoritative guidance covers a transaction, an entity looks first to guidance for similar transactions within the ASC, then to non-authoritative sources — but non-authoritative sources never override ASC content.

## Codification Address Format

Every piece of guidance lives at a precise address:

```
ASC  [Topic] - [Subtopic] - [Section] - [Paragraph]
      XXX       XX            XX          XX

Example:
ASC  606  -  10  -  25  -  1
     |        |     |      |
     |        |     |      Paragraph 1
     |        |     Recognition section (25)
     |        Overall subtopic (10)
     Revenue from Contracts with Customers
```

Sections follow a standardized numbering convention across all topics:

- **05** – Overview and Background  
- **15** – Scope  
- **20** – Glossary  
- **25** – Recognition  
- **30** – Initial Measurement  
- **35** – Subsequent Measurement  
- **45** – Other Presentation  
- **50** – Disclosure  

This consistency means once you learn the section map, you can navigate any topic predictably.

## Why a Deterministic Reference Matters

Because the CPA FAR exam tests specific codification citations and because accounting conclusions must be defensible in audits and financial statement reviews, knowing the *exact address* of a standard is not optional trivia — it is the mechanism by which preparers and auditors confirm they are applying the correct, current rule. An LLM or memory-based approach fails here because guidance is amended frequently; only a structured, versioned reference guarantees accuracy. Exam questions routinely ask which ASC topic governs a transaction, making address fluency a direct point-scoring skill.

## Prereqs

- Understanding of the role of FASB as the standard-setting body for U.S. GAAP  
- Familiarity with the concept of authoritative versus non-authoritative guidance  
- Basic awareness that financial statements are prepared under a recognized accounting framework  
- Recognition that the Codification replaced prior GAAP literature (SFASs, APB Opinions, etc.) effective 2009
